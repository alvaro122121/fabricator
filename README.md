## ⭐ About fabricator

Fabricator helps you pick the right files, check them against the main storage, and confirm they are correct. It is made for Windsurf Cascade users to manage artifacts with trust. You do not need to be a tech expert to use it.

## 🖥️ System Requirements

Before you start, make sure your computer meets these needs:

- Windows 10 or later (64-bit recommended)
- At least 4 GB of RAM
- 500 MB of free disk space
- Internet connection for downloading and verifying data

Having these will help fabricator run well on your system.

## ⚙️ How to Use fabricator

Once fabricator opens, you will see a simple menu. The main functions are:

- **Select Artifacts:** Choose the files or sets you want to work with.
- **Check Repo:** The app compares your choice with the official source to confirm accuracy.
- **Verify Evidence:** It ensures the data matches proof stored in the system.

### Selecting Artifacts

Click the "Choose Artifacts" button. A list or selection window will appear. Pick the items you want to include. You can select one or many depending on your project.

### Checking Repository Data

After choosing, press "Check Repo." fabricator looks for each artifact in the official record. It will alert you if something is missing or different.

### Verifying with Evidence

Next, hit "Verify." fabricator cross-checks the artifacts against stored evidence. This step confirms the files are correct and unchanged. The app will show a green check or a warning symbol.

## 📂 Where fabricator Saves Data

By default, fabricator saves your selected artifacts and settings in this location:

`C:\Users\<YourUserName>\Documents\FabricatorData`

You can change this folder inside the app settings.

## ❓ Troubleshooting

If fabricator does not open or crashes:

- Make sure your Windows is up to date.
- Check you have enough free disk space.
- Try running the app as administrator by right-clicking its icon and selecting "Run as administrator."
- Disable antivirus temporarily if it blocks the app.

If the problem persists, check the logs in the "Logs" folder inside FabricatorData for clues or ask someone with experience to help.
